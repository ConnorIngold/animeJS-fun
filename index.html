Fuck Internet Explorer

Why all web developer should stop developing for the IE.

For some this title seems like a no brainer. There are many who have used browsers such as Chrome and Firefox and cannot imagine returning to Internet Explorer (IE) ever again. But it’s not quite so simple for everyone. There are many users who do not have the ability to choose the browsers available to them in their workplace or they are using one of the many web apps that were built to be used in IE that has never been updated. So this post is for the system administrator (and anyone I build a website for) who needs convincing to update the company’s tools, the web app developer who should update their software or maybe even the user who has become complacent with IE.

The number one reason you should stop using IE is that it has major security issues. It is far more susceptible to hackers looking to discover the security software you are using. Once they know this they can attack your computer without you ever knowing. Microsoft releases bug fixes for IE on a fixed schedule which allows vulnerabilities to remain open for months before being addressed. Newer browsers are releasing new updates and security patches as needed and most of the time you are not even aware that your browser has been updated. This is because they understand how important your security is when browsing the Internet and have made it as simple as possible to keep you protected.

In February 2019, Microsoft released a blog titled “The Perils of Using Internet Explorer as Your Default Browser”. The author, a Microsoft employee, explains that IE is a “compatibility solution” and that they are not “supporting new web standards for it”. This explains why your brand new website does not look as expected in IE. Web developers are building and testing on today’s technology. And it’s not just your organization's website, its websites and web apps across the internet that you will miss out on if you do not move to a browser that meets today’s standards.
